Space Invaders

Coding with Adam is a YouTuber I have been watching on my coding journey. I've always loved old-school video games and spent my share of quarters and hours playing this game, although I got to say the Atari 2600 port is thoroughly my favorite version. I've wanted some more JavaScript tutorials to work on, as JS is challenging but rewarding. It can be Lots of files getting imported into other files. It gets called vanilla JS, but I don't know if learning JS isn't always vanilla or simple. I had never tried to make a game before, and since I love the old-school, this was a perfect tutorial to take a stab at. Shout out to Coding with Adam as they linked the Git Hub code in the video description, which helped as I replayed some parts and then went, "oh, that's what you're doing." The final game reminds me of a simple, early 2000s flash game that you play when the teacher in the library of the high school is like, "2 + 2 = 4 and research papers," and I'm just like, "yeah, I'm gonna play flash games and drive Sponge Bob's car around Bikini Bottom like I stole it" Simple online games rule! I want to make more online games.
